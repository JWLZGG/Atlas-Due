# Market Thesis

## Problem
Stablecoin settlement and treasury workflows increasingly depend on wallet-level trust decisions. Today, those decisions are often made manually across explorers, screenshots, spreadsheets, and internal notes. The process is slow, inconsistent, and hard to audit.

## Beachhead customer
- treasury teams
- payment operators
- stablecoin settlement workflows
- platforms onboarding payout or settlement wallets

## Insight
The market does not just need better wallet analytics. It needs portable review artifacts that make trust decisions easier to record, explain, and reuse.

## Why existing workflows are broken
- fragmented across tools
- difficult to standardize
- difficult to verify later
- trapped in internal documents
- not portable across workflows

## Why Solana
Solana gives Atlas Due:
1. wallet-native financial context for stablecoin and treasury activity
2. low-cost onchain review attestations that make diligence portable

## Why now
As stablecoin use and onchain treasury operations grow, trust infrastructure becomes more valuable. The need is shifting from “can we move funds onchain?” to “how do we review and trust the wallets involved?”

## Long-term market
Atlas Due starts with settlement and treasury wallet review, then expands into:
- counterparty review workflows
- issuer and treasury profiles
- payment-risk review infrastructure
- shared trust records for onchain finance
